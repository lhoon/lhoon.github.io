Projects

Ekko Duplex Transcript Analysis

Deakin Applied Artificial Intelligence Institute (A²I², formerly DSTIL)
Involvement: Project Manager, Delivery Lead

Ekko is a solution that supports customer service quality management. It is stateless, supporting on-prem or cloud deployment and employs Natural Language Processing and statistical analysis to examine agent and customer channel separated (duplex) transcripts. Ekko offers topic inference, sentiment scoring and statistical properties of transcripts and is designed to afford analysis from the agent, customer or conversation level view.

Maestro AI Platform (v1)

Deakin Applied Artificial Intelligence Institute (A²I², formerly DSTIL)
Involvement: Product Design, Product and Project Manager

Maestro, is a holistic Artificial Intelligence platform that can productionise a Jupyter Notebook into a live Amazon Web Service instance in minutes. Maestro also includes project management approaches that guide discovery and exploration. It also features data provenance and is our first effort to offer explainable Artificial Intelligence.

Pluto Financial Predictor

Deakin Software and Technology Innovation Lab (DSTIL)
Involvement: Solution Architecture, Delivery Lead

Pluto is a stateless micro service that employs Feature Extraction and Time Series analysis, ingesting financial observations to generate models for inference that predict catastrophic price drops in the next 24 hours. This system was our first foray into designing Data Science as a Service using an asynchronous queue architecture to for training and inference requests.

Extracting Signal from the Noise of App Reviews

Swinburne University of Technology

My thesis Extracting Signal from the Noise of App Reviews uses statistical and text analysis on 8.7 million app reviews to demonstrate that the majority of reviews, when read in isolation, do not yield developers much actionable feedback. Reviews are analysed to offer app benchmarking, search space reduction techniques and typical review properties to detect and sort useful reviews, toward informing project expectations, priorities and scoping.
