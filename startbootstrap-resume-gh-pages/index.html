Trauma Reception and Resuscitation

Swinburne NICTA Software Innovation Lab (SSIL)
Involvement: Researcher

The Trauma Reception and Resuscitation project is a Decision Support System built at the SSIL for The Alfred's Trauma Centre. This system is currently deployed in the Trauma Bays of The Alfred and also enables the trauma team the capability to enrich the algorithmic knowledge base of the system with minor training.

Users have noted the affordance for greater accountability during surgery. Additionally, the display of timeline since arrival and procedures performed provided the team members critical contextual information that augments the patient's current vital signs, especially when arriving in the bay separately.

Since deployment, an 80% reduction in blood administered to patients, 21% reduction in errors of omission and 3 hours less time spent in trauma have been observed. I produced one conference and one journal publication about affordances enabled by this system.

Extracting Signal from the Noise of App Reviews

Swinburne University of Technology

Brief abstract from my PhD thesis: Extracting Signal from the Noise of App Reviews: Apps stores offer a transparent feedback loop between users and developers via public app reviews and direct distribution of updates to users. Significant effort is expended to glean useful information from reviews and to examine this feedback loop. This thesis uses statistical and text analysis on 8.7 million reviews to demonstrate that the majority of reviews, when read in isolation, do not yield developers much actionable feedback. Reviews are analysed to offer app benchmarking, search space reduction techniques and typical review properties to detect and sort useful reviews, toward informing project expectations, priorities and scoping.
